This task is about farmers.


This is what a farm looks like:

Each square is one mile long.

All farms have pomegranate groves and pear groves.


For example, this grove has two pomegranate groves and one pear grove.


These farms are along a popular hiking trail, so hikers often walk past them and take some fruit.


The farmers share their fruit, but they prefer that hikers take because are out of season.

To do this, the farmers arrange huge boulders that hikers have to walk around.


Placing the boulders is a lot of work (each square is one mile long!) so the farmers always place the least number of boulders they think they need to.

The hikers always want to respect the farmer's preference.

Some hikers realize that the farmer arranged the boulders and some hikers don't.


Example 1: This hiker did realize that the farmer placed the boulders, so he chose the pears.


Example 2: This hiker did not realize that the farmer placed the rocks, so he chose the because walking around the boulders was too exhausting.


Different farmers have different beliefs about what hikers like. For example:

The farmer to the left believes hikers really like pomegranates and somewhat like pears. The farmer to the right believes hikers like pears but not pomegranates.

Different farmers also have different beliefs about how likely hikers are to realize that they placed the boulders. For example:

The farmer to the left believes hikers are highly likely to realize that they placed the boulders. The farmer to the left believes hikers can realize that they placed the boulders, but are more likely to think the boulders were placed naturally.

In this task, you will see different farms. In each trial, you will see how the farmer arranged the boulders, if they decided to place any at all. Below are examples of some of the farms you might see.


Your task is to figure out:

  1. What the farmer thinks the hikers like
  2. If the farmer expected hikers to realize that they placed the boulders, if they decided to place any at all

Remember:

  • Farmers find arranging boulders very exhausting, so they want to move as few as possible
  • Hikers want to walk the least amount possible
  • Hikers want to respect what the farmer prefers
